# SE Demo Quality Rubric
 
## How to use this file
When an SE shares a demo for review (recording link, deck, transcript, or live description), score it against the six criteria below. Each criterion is scored 1–3. Output the score per criterion, a total, a health indicator, 2–3 concrete strengths, and the 3 highest-impact improvements. Be specific — reference actual moments or slides, not general observations.
 
---
 
## Scoring criteria
 
### 1. Discovery alignment
Does the demo open with the customer's specific pain points, not with a generic VTEX intro?
 
- **3 — Strong:** Demo opens by referencing specific pain points surfaced in Discovery; the narrative is built around the customer's situation.
- **2 — Partial:** Customer context is mentioned but the demo quickly shifts to a standard VTEX walkthrough.
- **1 — Weak:** Generic demo with no reference to prior Discovery; could have been shown to any prospect.
### 2. Narrative & storyline
Is there a clear story connecting every section of the demo?
 
- **3 — Strong:** Clear story arc (situation → problem → VTEX solution) sustained throughout; transitions between sections are explicitly connected.
- **2 — Partial:** There is a structure, but transitions between sections are abrupt or unexplained.
- **1 — Weak:** Feature walkthrough with no connecting narrative; the audience must infer the relevance.
### 3. Industry relevance
Are examples, catalog data, and use cases from the customer's vertical?
 
- **3 — Strong:** All catalog items, screenshots, and use cases match the customer's exact vertical (e.g., pharma for pharma, apparel for apparel).
- **2 — Partial:** Similar vertical but not exact (e.g., general retail for a fashion brand); some generic data visible.
- **1 — Weak:** Demo uses default VTEX store data or a clearly unrelated vertical.
### 4. Technical credibility
Are the relevant technical capabilities demonstrated with sufficient depth?
 
- **3 — Strong:** Key technical requirements from Discovery are shown live, with enough depth to answer "how does this actually work."
- **2 — Partial:** Capabilities are mentioned or briefly shown but not demonstrated in a way that answers technical questions.
- **1 — Weak:** Only high-level features shown; no technical demonstration; relies entirely on slides or verbal claims.
### 5. Requirement coverage
Are all requirements identified in Discovery addressed during the demo?
 
- **3 — Strong:** Every requirement from Discovery is either demonstrated or explicitly acknowledged with a clear response (workaround, roadmap, partner).
- **2 — Partial:** Most requirements covered; some omitted without explanation.
- **1 — Weak:** Key requirements from Discovery are not covered or even mentioned.
### 6. Call to action
Does the demo end with a clear, concrete, and agreed next step?
 
- **3 — Strong:** Demo closes with a specific next step (action, date, owner) that the customer acknowledged or agreed to.
- **2 — Partial:** A next step is proposed but without customer commitment or a specific date.
- **1 — Weak:** Demo ends without a CTA, or with a vague one ("we'll send you more info").
---
 
## Total score interpretation
 
| Score | Health | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 15–18 | ✅ High quality | Ready to replicate as a reference demo |
| 10–14 | 🟡 Acceptable | Improvements needed before using as a standard |
| < 10 | 🔴 Needs revision | Do not replicate; address gaps before next demo |

## Notes for the agent
- Reference specific moments, slides, or timestamps when giving feedback — avoid generic comments.
- If you cannot evaluate a criterion due to missing information (e.g., no Discovery notes available), state what is missing rather than guessing.
- Strengths should be genuine and specific, not filler — if the demo scores low overall, it is acceptable to name only 1–2 real strengths.
