# SE Playbook — Recommended Next Steps
 
## How to use this file
When an SE asks for next steps on an opportunity, identify the current Rocketlane stage and the situation, then match it to the table below. Output a prioritized list — most urgent first — with a one-line rationale, the suggested owner, and a timeframe. Be specific: name the action, not the category.
 
---
 
## Decision table by stage & situation
 
| Stage | Situation | Recommended action | Owner | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Discovery | No Discovery Document | Create discovery doc and share with customer to validate pain points | SE Owner | 2 days |
| Discovery | No Opportunity Folder in Drive | Create folder using standard SE structure | SE Owner | 1 day |
| Discovery | Customer unresponsive > 7 days | Send follow-up with a value prop specific to their vertical and pain | SE Owner | 2 days |
| RFP | RFP received, no task assigned | Assign owner and run internal kick-off to analyze requirements | SE Owner | 1 day |
| RFP | RFP complex (> 20 requirements) | Escalate to SE Lead to prioritize key requirements before responding | SE Owner + SE Lead | 2 days |
| Demo | Demo not scheduled after 5 days in stage | Propose agenda with 2 date options to the customer champion | SE Owner | 1 day |
| Demo | Demo completed, no follow-up documented | Send recap email with agreed next steps and open items | SE Owner | 24 hours |
| Demo | Requirement not covered in demo | Schedule a dedicated technical deep-dive session or prepare a workaround doc | SE Owner | 3 days |
| POC | POC not started after 5 days | Confirm customer access and environment setup | SE Owner | 1 day |
| POC | Technical blocker > 3 days unresolved | Escalate to SE Lead / Product with full blocker context | SE Owner | 1 day |
| POC | Customer inactive in POC | Check in with champion to re-confirm priority and unblock | SE Owner | 2 days |
| Solution Design | Architecture not defined | Schedule an internal Architecture Review session | SE Owner + SE Lead | 3 days |
| Solution Design | PS proposal not sent | Coordinate with PS Lead for scoping and effort estimation | SE Owner + PS Lead | 3 days |

| Closing | Handover not scheduled | Coordinate handover session with PS team | SE Owner + PS Lead | 2 days |
| Any | Due date passed | Update due date in Rocketlane and log the reason for delay | SE Owner | Immediate |
| Any | Status inconsistent with progress | Update project status in Rocketlane to reflect actual state | SE Owner | Immediate |
 
---
 
## Escalation triggers
- Opportunity in 🔴 critical for more than 7 consecutive days → escalate to SE Lead.
- Enterprise account (ACV > $500K USD) with no activity for more than 10 days → bring SE Lead into the loop.
- Technical blocker unresolved for more than 5 days → involve Product or Solutions Architect.
- Customer unresponsive for more than 21 days at any stage → reassess opportunity priority with SE Lead.
---
 
## Good progression signals
- Each stage has at least one artifact completed before moving to the next.
- Activity (task update or Rocketlane log) at least every 7 days.
- Customer champion engaged and responding within 5 business days.
- No stage takes more than 30 days without a deliberate hold decision logged.
---
 
## Notes for the agent
- Always recommend the most specific action available given the data — avoid generic suggestions like "follow up with customer."
- If multiple next steps apply, prioritize by urgency (due date proximity, days without activity) then by impact (stage-blocking vs. administrative).
- When an escalation trigger is met, include it explicitly in the output with the triggering condition.
